About Our Company

Founded on the principles of precision and innovation, our company began with a single vision: to transform how industries view their assets. What started as a passion for aviation technology quickly evolved into a premier thermal and aerial inspection service. We realized early on that drones offer more than just a bird's-eye view—they provide critical data that helps businesses operate more efficiently and safely. Over the years, we have invested heavily in top-tier thermal imaging equipment and pilot training to ensure we deliver the highest quality results for every client.

Our mission is to make the unseen visible and the dangerous accessible. We are committed to integrity, safety, and excellence in every flight we undertake. Whether we are helping a homeowner identify heat loss or assisting a large industrial complex with preventative maintenance, we approach every project with the same level of dedication. We believe in building lasting relationships with our clients through transparency and actionable insights, ensuring that you have the information you need to make smart, data-driven decisions.

Frequently Asked Questions

Thermal imaging detects infrared radiation (heat) emitted by objects. It is used to identify temperature variations that indicate issues such as water leaks, missing insulation, electrical hot spots, solar panel defects, and HVAC inefficiencies. It allows us to "see" problems that are invisible to the naked eye.

The duration depends on the size and complexity of the site. A standard residential roof inspection can take 30-60 minutes, while large commercial or industrial sites may require several hours or multiple days. We plan our missions efficiently to minimize disruption to your operations.

For safety and data quality, we cannot fly in heavy rain, snow, or high winds (usually above 20mph). For thermal inspections specifically, we also require specific environmental conditions—often flying at dawn or dusk—to ensure the best thermal contrast between materials.

After the flight, we process the data and deliver a comprehensive digital report. This typically includes high-resolution images, annotated thermal maps, and a summary of findings. Data is securely delivered via a cloud link for easy download and sharing with your team.
